Soccer[1]
Also known as football in most parts of the world, is the most popular sport globally, played by millions of people of all ages. It is a team sport that involves two teams of eleven players each, who compete to score goals by getting a ball into the opposing team's net. The game is governed by a set of rules known as the Laws of the Game.
[1] Basic Rules:-
  1. Objective: The main objective is to score more goals than the opponent within the match time.
  2. Duration: A standard match consists of two halves of 45 minutes each, with a 15-minute halftime break.
  3. The Field: Matches are played on a rectangular field with a goal at each end. The dimensions can vary but typically range around 100-110 meters in length and 64-75 meters in width.
  4. The Ball: The game is played with a spherical ball, usually made of leather or other suitable materials.
  5. Players: Each team has 11 players, including one goalkeeper who is the only player allowed to handle the ball with their hands within the penalty area.
[1] Game Play:-
  • Kickoff: The game starts with a kickoff from the center of the field.
  • Scoring: A goal is scored when the ball completely crosses the goal line between the goalposts and beneath the crossbar.
  • Passing and Dribbling: Players pass the ball to teammates and dribble (run with the ball) to advance towards the opponent's goal.
  • Defending: The opposing team attempts to block the ball, tackle players, and intercept passes to prevent goals.
  • Offside Rule: A player is offside if they are nearer to the opponent's goal than both the ball and the second-last opponent when the ball is played to them, unless they are in their half of the field.

Ping Pong[2]
Also known as table tennis, is a fast-paced and popular indoor sport played by two or four players. Originating in England in the early 20th century, it is now a globally recognized game, both as a recreational activity and a competitive sport.
[2] Basic Rules:-
  1. Objective: The main objective is to score points by hitting the ball over the net and into the opponent's half of the table in a way that the opponent cannot return it.
  2. The Table: The game is played on a rectangular table that measures 2.74 meters in length, 1.525 meters in width, and 0.76 meters in height. A net divides the table into two equal halves.
  3. The Ball: The ball is small, lightweight, and typically made of celluloid or similar plastic material.
  4. The Paddles: Players use paddles (also called bats or rackets) covered with rubber to hit the ball.
[2] Game Play:-
  • Service: The game starts with a serve, where the server must hit the ball to bounce once on their side of the table before crossing the net and bouncing on the opponent's side.
  • Rallies: Players hit the ball back and forth, trying to make it difficult for the opponent to return.
  • Scoring: A point is scored if the opponent fails to return the ball, hits it out of bounds, or commits a fault. Games are usually played to 11 points, with a required margin of at least two points to win.
  • Matches: A match typically consists of the best of five or seven games.
